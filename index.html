Clear guidance when it matters most

Interactive prototypes. Tap any card to open the flow. These are working previews of how HELORA behaves in the moments they're built for.

Screen
Home Screen

The starting point — the calm entry to everything else, and the five-option hub for when you can't talk.

 Scenario
Safe Journey

Michael heads out for the evening and shares his route. Laney watches quietly from home, seeing each leg pass by — nothing to get excited about, just a promise kept until he's home safe.

 Screen
Need Help Now

Starts a 911 call with a short pre-connect window — Talk, Can't Talk, or Quick Reach. Staying silent safely proceeds on its own.

 Scenario
I Can't Talk

For a moment when speaking aloud isn't safe or possible. HELORA moves to Silent Mode — large, simple choices, and the option to stay quiet or sound an alarm.

 Screen
Quick Reach

Reaches your trusted contacts in one tap, and shows you honestly which messages actually went through.

 Screen
Responder View

What a paramedic or nurse sees — your critical care details first, family and continuity second.

 Scenario
Rebecca Demo

Rebecca falls in the middle of the night and can't get up. HELORA guides her through the 911 call, holds her location ready, and helps her let her people know — then gives her a moment to breathe while help is on the way.

 Scenario
Remote Emergency

A hiker on Rusk Mountain is caught ahead of a fast-moving wildfire, far from any street address. HELORA shows the terrain, their exact location, and the conditions around them — the ground truth a street address can't give a dispatcher.
Preview build. HELORA coaches and stages around a 911 call — it does not replace emergency services.
